advantages and disadvanatages of big data

The increase in the amount of data available presents both opportunities and problems. In general, obtaining more data about customers (and potential customers) should allow companies to better customize their products and marketing efforts in order to create the highest level of satisfaction and repeat business. Companies that are able to collect a large amount of data are given the opportunity to conduct a deeper and richer analysis. While better analysis is positive, big data can also lead to increased load and noise. Companies should be able to handle larger amounts of data, specifying data that represents signals compared to noise. Determining what makes the relevant data become a major factor. Moreover, the nature and form of data may require special processing before action is taken. Structured data, consisting of numerical values, can be stored and sorted easily. Unstructured data, such as emails, videos, and text documents, may require more sophisticated technologies to be applied before they become useful.
